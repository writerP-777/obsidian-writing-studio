# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Obsidian Writing Studio is a software project. We are here to build something useful together. Every contributor — regardless of background, experience level, or personal beliefs — deserves to be evaluated on the quality of their work and the character of their conduct, nothing else.

We commit to maintaining project spaces where honest, constructive, and respectful collaboration can happen. This document defines the conduct standards that make that possible.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected behavior

- Communicate honestly and respectfully, even in disagreement
- Give and receive constructive feedback without taking it personally
- Stay focused on the work — the code, the documentation, the issue at hand
- Assume good faith in others unless there is clear evidence otherwise
- Respect that contributors come from different backgrounds and hold different beliefs

### Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or sustained personal attacks — in public or in private
- Insults, mockery, or derogatory comments directed at a person
- Publishing someone's private information (home address, email, phone number, etc.) without their explicit consent
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome advances of any kind
- Deliberately disrupting discussions or derailing issue threads
- Retaliating against anyone who reports a conduct concern in good faith

---

## Scope

This code of conduct applies within all official project spaces: the GitHub repository, issue tracker, pull requests, discussions, and any communication channels associated with the project.

It also applies when a contributor is acting as a representative of the project in public (for example, posting on behalf of the project or speaking at an event).

**What this code does not govern:** Contributors' beliefs, convictions, or views on contested social, religious, or political questions are their own. Holding such views — provided they are not expressed in project spaces in ways that harass, target, or demean specific individuals — is not a violation of this policy. This project evaluates contributors by their conduct here, not by their convictions elsewhere.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying these standards and responding to reports of violations. They will act with fairness and consistency, and will communicate their reasoning when taking moderation action.

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions — including comments, commits, issues, and pull requests — that violate these standards.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code of conduct, you may:

- Open a GitHub issue with the title prefix **[Conduct]**, or
- Contact the maintainer directly via the contact information in the repository profile

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with discretion. The privacy of the person reporting will be respected throughout the process.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will apply the following graduated response based on the nature and severity of the violation:

| Level | Situation | Response |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **1 — Correction** | Minor or first-time lapse in professional conduct | Private written clarification of the standard and how it was violated |
| **2 — Warning** | A more serious incident, or a repeated pattern of minor lapses | Formal warning with defined expectations for future conduct |
| **3 — Temporary Ban** | Sustained misconduct or a serious single incident | Temporary removal from all project spaces for a defined period |
| **4 — Permanent Ban** | Continued violations after warnings, or severe misconduct | Permanent removal from the project community |

Maintainers will use good judgment in applying these levels. The goal is correction and restoration where possible, not punishment for its own sake.

---

## Appeals

If you believe a moderation decision was applied unfairly, you may contact the maintainer to request a review. State your concern clearly and specifically. Decisions will be reconsidered in good faith.
